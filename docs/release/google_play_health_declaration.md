# Google Play Health Apps Declaration Draft

Use this draft when completing the Health apps declaration in Play Console.

## Recommended Positioning

Select the category that best matches a wellness / health-and-fitness style app, not a medical or regulated clinical product.

Suggested framing:

- wellness and healthy routine support
- hydration, sleep, mood, weight, notes, and habit consistency
- general product guidance in a non-medical lifestyle context

## What The App Does

- onboarding around wellness goals and habits
- 7 / 14 / 21-day habit-oriented plans
- tracking of water, sleep, weight, mood, notes, streaks, and adherence
- reminder notifications
- general wellness-oriented product catalog and outbound links to an external merchant site

## What The App Does Not Do

- does not diagnose
- does not treat
- does not cure
- does not prevent disease
- does not prescribe
- does not interpret tracked data as medical advice
- does not position itself as a medical device

## Sensitive Health Positioning Guardrails

Use these explanations if the declaration asks how the app avoids harmful or misleading health behavior:

- the app uses non-medical wellness wording
- disclaimer and consent are presented before primary use
- legal screens are accessible in app
- product guidance is framed as optional general wellness support only
- users mentioning symptoms, disease, medication interactions, pregnancy concerns, or severe reactions are directed to qualified healthcare professionals

## If Asked About Health Data

Suggested explanation:

The app stores self-entered wellness tracking data such as water intake, sleep, weight, mood, and notes to support daily routine tracking. The app does not provide diagnosis, clinical interpretation, treatment instructions, or disease-management functionality.
